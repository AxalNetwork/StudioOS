Once the **Axal Admin** reviews the profile captured by your chatbot, they will propose a specific legal "Closing Binder." Since Axal operates as a **programmable venture studio**, these agreements are often standardized templates triggered by the **Legal Engine**.

Here is what the admin will propose for each profile type:

---

### 1. The Investor Profile
Investors are categorized by their "check size" and commitment type.

| Sub-Profile | Admin Proposed Agreement | Key Engagement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Limited Partner (LP)** | **Subscription Booklet & LPA** | Committing capital to the main VC fund for a 7–10 year term. |
| **Syndicate Partner** | **SPV Joinder Agreement** | Investing in a specific "Spin-Out" subsidiary on a deal-by-deal basis. |
| **Co-Investor** | **Co-Investment Side Letter** | Aligning terms between Axal and an external VC firm for a joint round. |

---

### 2. The Operator / Advisor Profile
For those providing "sweat equity" or specialized expertise to the projects.

* **Venture Partner / Advisor:** The admin will propose a **Venture Share Agreement** (often based on the **FAST**—Founder Advisor Standard Template). This defines the vesting schedule for equity in the Holding Company or specific subsidiaries.
* **Operating Partner:** A **Master Service Agreement (MSA)** with an **Equity-for-Services** rider. This allows the operator to bill the Management Company while earning a "carry" in the fund.

---

### 3. The Legal & Service Provider Profile
These partners provide the "Infrastructure" for the 30-day spin-outs.

* **Preferred Legal Counsel:** An **Engagement Letter** that defines fixed-fee structures for "Spin-Out Packages" (Incorporation, IP Transfer, SAFE issuance).
* **Technical Partner:** A **White-Label Service Agreement**. This allows the partner to integrate their MVP development tools directly into the **StudioOS Legal Engine**.

---

### 4. The Project Founder Profile
For entrepreneurs entering the "30-Day Spin-Out Engine."

* **Founder Collaboration Agreement:** Signed during the initial "Validated Idea" phase. It defines the IP ownership (held by Axal Holding Co) and the success metrics for the decision gate.
* **Spin-Out Subsidiary SPA:** Once the project passes the "Gate," the admin proposes the **Stock Purchase Agreement** and **IP License/Transfer**, officially creating the new Delaware entity with the founder as a major shareholder.

---

### 5. The Liquidity Profile
Focused on the "Returns" arrow of the Axal flowchart.

* **Secondary Purchase Agreement (SPA):** Proposed when an early LP or Founder wants to sell their vested shares to a secondary fund before a full exit.
* **M&A Advisory Mandate:** A contract for investment banks or brokers to begin the "Exit" process for a successful subsidiary.

---

### How the Admin Sends These (The "Trigger")
In your **Replit/Cloudflare** system, once the Admin clicks **"Verify Profile"** in the dashboard:
1.  **D1 Update:** The database changes `admin_status` to `'verified'`.
2.  **Document Generation:** The **Legal Engine** (integrated via an API like HelloSign or DocuSign) automatically drafts the relevant agreement using the data captured by the AI (Legal Name, EIN, Signatory).
3.  **Email Notification:** The user receives a link to sign their "Axal Partnership Binder."

> **Axal Pro-Tip:** Since Axal is a Delaware LLC, all these agreements will include a **"Choice of Law"** clause pointing to the State of Delaware, ensuring all partners operate under the same legal framework regardless of their physical location.
