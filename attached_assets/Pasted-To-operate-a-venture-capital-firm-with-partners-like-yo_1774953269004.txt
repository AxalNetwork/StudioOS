To operate a venture capital firm with partners—like your **Axal.vc** project—you need a legal stack that covers three distinct "layers": the management of the firm itself, the relationship with your investors (LPs), and the investments you make into startups.

Since you are working with partners, the **internal governance** is the most critical starting point to prevent future "divorce" issues.

### 1. Internal Management Layer (The GP Level)
This is the "brain" of the operation. These documents define how you and your partners make decisions, split profits (carried interest), and what happens if someone leaves.

* **Operating Agreement (LLC) or Partnership Agreement (LP):** This is your internal "constitution." It details voting power, capital contributions, and the "buy-sell" provisions for partners.
* **Founder/Partnership Agreement:** Specifically outlines the vesting of **Carried Interest** (the 20% profit share). Partners usually vest their carry over 4–10 years to ensure long-term commitment.
* **Employment or Service Agreements:** Defines the roles, responsibilities, and salaries (from the management fee) for each partner.
* **Investment Committee (IC) Charter:** A formal document describing the process for approving a deal. Does it require a unanimous vote or a simple majority?

### 2. Fund Formation Layer (The LP Level)
If you are raising a pool of capital from outside investors (Limited Partners) to invest under the Axal.vc brand, you need:

* **Limited Partnership Agreement (LPA):** The main contract between the VC (General Partner) and the investors (Limited Partners). It sets the "2 and 20" fee structure and the fund's lifespan (usually 10 years).
* **Private Placement Memorandum (PPM):** A disclosure document that outlines the risks, strategy, and legal nuances of the fund for potential investors.
* **Subscription Agreement:** The "order form" an investor signs to legally commit a specific dollar amount to your fund.
* **Management Company Agreement:** A contract where the Fund hires your Management Company (Axal.vc) to run the day-to-day operations.

### 3. Investment Execution Layer (The Portfolio Level)
These are the templates shown in your screenshot. These are used when Axal.vc actually sends money to a startup.

* **SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity):** Best for early-stage/pre-seed. It’s fast and avoids immediate valuation talks.
* **Term Sheet:** A non-binding summary of the main points of a deal (Valuation, Board Seats, Protective Provisions).
* **Stock Purchase Agreement (SPA):** The heavy-duty contract for "Priced Rounds" (Series A, etc.) where you are buying actual shares.
* **Voting & Investors’ Rights Agreements:** Ensures Axal.vc has a say in major company decisions (e.g., selling the company or hiring/firing the CEO).

### 4. Compliance & Regulatory (The "Must-Haves")
* **Form ADV:** If you are in the US, you likely need to register as an **Exempt Reporting Adviser (ERA)** with the SEC.
* **AML/KYC Policy:** Procedures to verify that your partners and investors aren't involved in money laundering.
* **Section 83(b) Election:** A critical tax form for partners to file within 30 days of receiving their equity/carry to avoid massive future tax bills.

---
> **Axal VC Tip:** If you're just starting out with partners and no outside capital yet, focus on your **Operating Agreement** and an **Equity Split Agreement**. Don't spend $50k on a full Fund LPA until you have committed interest from LPs.
